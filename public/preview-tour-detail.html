Blue Cave & Hvar Island — Full Day

Duration
Full Day · ~10 hrs
Departs
07:30 AM
Location
Hvar · Vis Island
Price
€130 / person
About this experience

The sea inside the Blue Cave on Vis Island turns a supernatural luminescent blue — a natural phenomenon caused by sunlight refracting through an underwater opening. It stops people mid-sentence. After that, Hvar, shown to you the way locals actually experience it: narrow stone lanes, a terrace nobody from TripAdvisor has found, cold wine by the water.

The day covers five islands in the Adriatic — Biševo, Vis, Budikovac lagoon, and Pakleni — with swimming stops along the way. We know the right boat, the right guide, and the right time to arrive at the cave before the crowds.

What to bring

  • Swimsuit and towel
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
  • Light windbreaker or long sleeves (the boat ride is fast)
  • Water shoes — the Blue Lagoon has a rocky bottom
  • Cash for the Blue Cave entrance fee (€18–24, not included)
  • Camera or waterproof phone case
  • Snacks and water for the journey

Good to know

Swimming inside the Blue Cave is not permitted — you view it from the boat
Not recommended for pregnant women or people with serious back problems (speedboat rides can be bumpy)
Not recommended for children under 3 years old
Blue Cave entrance fee paid on-site in cash only (€18 Apr–Jun & Sep–Oct / €24 Jul–Aug)
Tour departs regardless of light rain — cancelled only in severe weather, full refund applies
Snorkelling equipment included
Price from
€130 / person
Blue Cave entrance fee included. All islands, all stops, one price.

Duration
~8–9 Hours
Departs
07:45 AM
Location
Šibenik Region
Price
€35 / person
About this experience

Swim beneath waterfalls an hour from your door. The Krka River has carved a series of cascades through untouched Mediterranean woodland — the kind of place you assumed only existed in films. We know the right entry point, the right time to arrive, and how to avoid the tour-bus rush. Lunch at a local village and a stop in Šibenik on the way back.

What to bring

  • Swimsuit and towel
  • Comfortable walking shoes — pathways can be slippery
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
  • Bottle of water
  • Cash for personal expenses
  • Camera

Good to know

Swimming permitted only in designated areas (Roški Slap, Stinice, Pisak) from 1 June to 30 September
Drones are strictly prohibited inside the park — confiscation and fines apply
Tour runs in light rain (ponchos provided) — cancelled only in severe weather
National park entrance fee paid separately on arrival (not included)
No lunch included — plenty of options inside the park and in Šibenik
